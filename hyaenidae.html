Classification

Hyaenidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Feliformia", which encompasses all catlike carnivorans, characterized by a shorter snout than caniforms. Other families of Feliformia include Felidae, Prionodontidae, and Nandiniidae. Furthermore, Hyaenidae belongs to Viverroidea, which encompasses families closely related genetically, those being Viverridae, Herpestidae, Eupleridae, and itself.

Description

Hyaenidae, also known as hyenas, are tall, short-bodied feliforms with a significant sloping arched back. They are aggressive scavengers, and as such they have teeth and jaws designed for eating coarse food and crushing bones. Their skulls resemble those of dogs but are heavier and shorter, and their necks are thick and short. They have long front legs and short hind legs. They have four toes on each foot with non-retractable claws. They have anal glands with strong-smelling secretions.
