Classification

Felidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Feliformia", which encompasses all catlike carnivorans, characterized by a shorter snout than caniforms. Other families of Feliformia include Viverridae, Hyaenidae, and Herpestidae. Furthermore, Felidae belongs to Feloidea, which encompasses families closely related genetically, those being only Prionodontidae and Felidae.

Description

Felids, also known as cats, have slender muscular bodies and powerful limbs. They all have five toes on their front paws and four on the hind paws. They walk on their toes, and have sharp retractible claws used to grab and grip prey. They have round skulls and shortened snouts. They have rough papillae on their tongue and sensitive taste organs in their mouths. They have large eyes that are strong in the dark, as well as highly sensitive whiskers to navigate when sight is unreliable.
