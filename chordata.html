Classification

Chordata is a phylum in the group "Eumetozoa" which are characterized by the presence of specialized tissues, neurons, and muscles. Other organisms in this subkingdom include Comb Jellies, Jellyfish, and Anenome. Additionally, chordates belong to the group "Bilateria", characterized by bilateral symmetry, a head, tail, belly, and back, and a complete digestive system. Other bilaterials include molluscs and arthropods. Furthermore, Chordata is in the group "Deuterostomia", meaning their anus forms before their mouth. Other deuterosomes include starfish and acorn worms.

Description

Chordates are characterized as animals that have, at some point in their development, all of the following anatomical structures: the dorsal nerve cord (hollow tube responsible for coordination and development), the notochord (a cartilagenous rod responsible for directional reference in development and as a precursor to the axial endoskeleton), the pharyngeal slits (gills either used for respiration or feeding), the thyroid, and a post-anal tail.
