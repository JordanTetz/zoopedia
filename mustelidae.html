Classification

Mustelidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Caniformia", which encompasses all doglike carnivorans, characterized by a longer snout than feliforms. Other families of Caniformia include Canidae, Phocidae, and Ursidae. Additionally, Mustelidae belongs to Musteloidea, the super family that unites caniform families based on similarities in the skull and teeth. The other members in this family are Procyonidae, Ailuridae, and Mephitidae. Musteloidea is most closely related to the Pinniped group.

Description

Mustelids are carnivorans with a long body, long tail, small head, and short legs. Their spinal columns are very flexible, allowing them to be nimble and agile, often undergound where they are designed to hunt. They have five toes on each foot, with unrectractable claws and either walk flat on their feet or or on their toes. They lack a clavicle which allows more flexibility for their limbs. They have developed canine and cheek teeth, and their molars are designed to crush food items. Strong muscles in their jaw and around their skull give them one of the strongest pound-for-pound bite forces of any mammal. One of their distinct features is their various scent glands used to mark territory.
