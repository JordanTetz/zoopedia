Classification

Procyonidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Caniformia", which encompasses all doglike carnivorans, characterized by a longer snout than feliforms. Other families of Caniformia include Canidae, Phocidae, and Ursidae. Additionally, Procyonidae belongs to Musteloidea, the super family that unites caniform families based on similarities in the skull and teeth. The other members in this family are Mustelidae, Ailuridae, and Mephitidae. Musteloidea is most closely related to the Pinniped group.

Description

Procyonidae is the family that includes all raccoonlike carnivorans. Procyonids tend to be small with slender bodies and long, bushy tails. Their faces are relatively short, broad faces. They have short but erect ears. They have distinctive markings on the face and tail. They walk flat on their paws, with each one having five digits which they sometimes use to grasp food or tree branches.They are generally less suited to hunting prey, with poorly developped canine teeth. They are omnivorous, eating both plants and animals.
