Classification

Canidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Caniformia", which encompasses all doglike carnivorans, characterized by a longer snout than feliforms. Other families of Caniformia include Ursidae, Phocidae, and Mustelidae.

Description

Canids usually have limbrer bodies and long bushy tails. They have long legs and stand on their toes. They walk on cushioned pads and their thumb is raised from the ground. Most have five toes on the front legs and four toes on the hind legs. Canine muzzles are elongated and the tip of the nose is bare, giving them a hightened sense of smell.
