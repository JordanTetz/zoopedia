Classification

Perissodactyla belongs to the group of mammals known as "Placentialia", characterized by the prescence of the placenta, an organ that nourishes the fetus during development. Other placental orders include Primates, Rodentia, and Proboscidea. Additionally, Perissodactyla belongs to a group of placentals known as "Laurasitheria", united together by similar gene sequences. Other orders in Laurasiatheria include Chiroptera, Carnivora, and Eulipotyphla. Lastly, Perissodactyla belongs to the group called "Ungulata", which consists of large animals with hooves. The only other order in this group is Artiodactyla.

Description

Perissodactyla, also known as the odd-toed ungulates, defines the group of ungulates with one or three weight-bearing toes. All ungulates stand on the tips of their toes, which requires a shorter, thicker humerus bones. Odd-toed ungulates have long upper jaws and large lower jaws. They have small or absent canines and incisors, large molars, and a distinctive gap between their front teeth and cheek teeth. Odd-toed ungulates have a large cecum (a portion of the intestine), where their bulky vegetable diet is digested by bacteria.
