Classification

Phocidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Caniformia", which encompasses all doglike carnivorans, characterized by a longer snout than feliforms. Other families of Caniformia include Canidae, Ursidae, and Mustelidae. Additionally, Phocidae belongs to a group known as the "Pinnipedia", which includes all marine, fin-footed caniforms. The other families in this group are Otariidae and Odobenidae.

Description

Phocidae, also known as earless seals, are a group of marine carnivorans specialized to survive in the cold ocean. Like all pinnipeds, they have streamlined, flexible bodies with slick fur and limbs modified into flippers for maximum movement capabilities in the water. They have large eye orbits, short snouts, and less teeth than normal for carnivorans. They also have blubber under their skin to keep them warm. More specifically, earless seals are generally larger, lack hind limb mobility on land, and as their name suggests, do not have external ears.
