Classification

Animalia is a kingdom of the domain "Eukaryota", which means that their cells have a nucleus. Other Eukaryotes include plants and fungi.

Description

All animals are multicellular and heterotrophic, meaning they cannot produce their own food and must instead digest organic material internally. Additionally, all animals require oxygen to breathe and are motile in at least one stage of their life cycle. Cell differentiation as an embryo is unique to animals due to the stage of embryonic development known as the blastula. Nearly all animals reproduce sexually.
