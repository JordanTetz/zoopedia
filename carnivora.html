Classification

Carnivora belongs to the group of mammals known as "Placentialia", characterized by the prescence of the placenta, an organ that nourishes the fetus during development. Other placental orders include Primates, Rodentia, and Proboscidea. Additionally, carnivora belongs to a group of placentals known as "Laurasitheria", united together by similar gene sequences. Other orders in Laurasiatheria include Chiroptera, Artiodactyla, and Perissodactyla.

Description

Carnivora is an order of mammals who have evolved and are specialised to hunt and eat meat. They have long, pointed teeth that can cut meat with scissor-like motion. Their braincase, the part of the skull that houses the brain, is round and developed. All carnivorans are quadrupedal and lack a clavicle. The front legs always have five digits while the back legs can have four or five. All carnivorans have claws, that in most cases, are used to catch and hold prey.
