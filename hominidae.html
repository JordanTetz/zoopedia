Classifiction

Hominidae belongs to the suborder "Haplorhini" or the dry-nosed primates. Other families in this suborder include Tarsiidae, Callitrichidae, and Atelidae. Additionally, hominids belong to the infraorder "Catarrhini" also known as the Old-World monkeys and apes. One family in this infraorder is Cercopithecidae. Lastly, Hominidae belongs to the parvorder "Hominoidea", also known as the apes, which include only Hylobatidae and itself.

Description

Hominids, also known as the great apes, are the largest primates in the world. They have erect posture, robust builds, and lack a tail. All great apes can walk bipedally, though most can only do this for a short time. Their nostrils are close together and downward-facing. They have broad incisors and underdevelopped canine teeth. All great apes have large braincases which gives them superior intelligence, allowing them to use tools and have complex social groups.
