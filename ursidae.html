Classification

Ursidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Caniformia", which encompasses all doglike carnivorans, characterized by a longer snout than feliforms. Other families of Caniformia include Canidae, Phocidae, and Mustelidae.

Description

Bears have bulky bodies with stocky legs and strong, thick bones. They have large clavicles and pelvises to support their weight. They have small rounded ears, thick blubber, and shaggy fur to minimize heat loss. They have powerful arms and large paws on which they walk flat, each with 5 long claws. They have long snouts which gives them an excellent sense of smell. They also have a large skull with powerful jaw muscles.
