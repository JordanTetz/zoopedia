Classification

Mephitidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Caniformia", which encompasses all doglike carnivorans, characterized by a longer snout than feliforms. Other families of Caniformia include Canidae, Phocidae, and Ursidae. Additionally, Mephitidae belongs to Musteloidea, the super family that unites caniform families based on similarities in the skull and teeth. The other members in this family are Mustelidae, Ailuridae, and Procyonidae. Musteloidea is most closely related to the Pinniped group.

Description

Mephitidae is the group of carnivorans that includes skunks and stink badgers. They have small bodies with short legs, a large bushy tail, and a small cone-shaped head. They have distinctive patterns, usually spots or stripes, which serve to warn predators if they get too close. If that doesn't work, they have disinctive scent glands used to spray strong-scelling liquid to scare the predators away. They use their strong claws to dig and find food. They are nocturnal omnivores.
