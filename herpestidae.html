Classification

Herpestidae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Feliformia", which encompasses all catlike carnivorans, characterized by a shorter snout than caniforms. Other families of Feliformia include Felidae, Prionodontidae, and Nandiniidae. Furthermore, Herpestidae belongs to Viverroidea, which encompasses families closely related genetically, those being Hyaenidae, Viverridae, Eupleridae, and itself.

Description

Herpestidae, also known as mongooses, have long faces and bodies with short legs and long tails. They have short, flat ears and narrow ovular pupils, much like those of goats. They have five toes on each foot and their claws are non-retractable, mostly being used for digging. They have long needle-like incisor teeth capable of puncturing bones. They have large anal scent glands. They also have a rare mutation that protects them against snake venom.
