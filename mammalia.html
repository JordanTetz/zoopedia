Classification

Mammals belong to the subphylum "Vertebrata", which consists of chordates with cartilagenous or bony backbones. Vertebrates include sharks, ray-finned fish, and tetrapods. The superclass "Tetrapoda" describes vertebrates four limbs and digits or vertebrates with ancestors with these structures. Tetrapods include amphibians, sauropsids, and mammals.

Description

Mammals are characterized by several universal or nearly universal features. All mammals have fur at some point in their life cycle. Mammals use mammary glands to provide nutrition for their young. All mammals are also warm blooded. They have one bone in the lower jaw, three bones in the middle ear, four chambers in the heart, and a highly advanced diaphragm.
