Classification

Viverridae belongs the group of carnivorans known as "Feliformia", which encompasses all catlike carnivorans, characterized by a shorter snout than caniforms. Other families of Feliformia include Felidae, Prionodontidae, and Nandiniidae. Furthermore, Viverridae belongs to Viverroidea, which encompasses families closely related genetically, those being Hyaenidae, Herpestidae, Eupleridae, and itself.

Description

Viverrids, also known as civets, are arboreal feliforms adapted to climbing and living in trees. They have long slender bodies and long tails. Their legs are shorter, with five toes on each foot and semi-retractable claws. Their noses are tapered and their ears are short but erect. Viverrid teeth, especially the cheek teeth, are underdeveloped. Most species are nocturnal hunters that eat small animals and insects.
