Classification

Primates belongs to the group of mammals known as "Placentialia", characterized by the prescence of the placenta, an organ that nourishes the fetus during development. Other placental orders include Carnivora, Artiodactyla, and Proboscidea. Additionally, carnivora belongs to a group of placentals known as "Euarchontaglires", categorized by similarities in DNA sequences. Other members of this group include Rodentia, Lagomorphs, Scandentia, and Dermoptera.

Description

Most primates have evolved to be masters of the arboreal environment. Thus, their hands are very good at grasping and most have opposable thumbs. Every primate has five inward-facing digits, as well as fingernails and sensitive pads on each fingertip. Primates have enlarged clavicles, giving their shoulders broad motion while brachiating. Sight is the primary sense for primates and, as such, they have forward-facing eyes and binocular vision (useful in depth percetpion). Primates therefore have reduced snouts because smell has less significance. Primateshave larger brains than usual for their body size, and many live in complex social groups.
